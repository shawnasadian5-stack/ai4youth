01 · Our Mission

Building community by building
young thinkers.

AI is reshaping how a generation learns, writes, and thinks - often faster than schools can keep up.

AI 4 Youth exists to fill that gap. We're university students who have lived through the transition our high schoolers are about to make, and we believe young people deserve honest, balanced guidance about a technology that will shape their academic and professional lives.

This isn't a workshop about banning AI, and it isn't a celebration of it either. It's a community-driven conversation - student to student - about what these tools are good for, where they fail, and how to use them in ways that build skill rather than replace it.

i.

Empower local youth

Equip students of every age - with a particular focus on those entering Grade 11 and 12 - with the critical thinking habits they'll need to navigate university, where AI policies are stricter and expectations for original thought are higher.

ii.

Serve our community

Bring free, in-person AI literacy programming to North Shore schools - meeting students and educators where they are, in classrooms across the community we grew up in.

iii.

Educate ethically

Address one of the most sensitive and fast-moving topics in education with honesty, research-backed evidence, and the understanding that AI is a tool - not a substitute for learning.

02 · The Urgency

Why this matters now.

AI tools arrived faster than the guidance on how to use them. The result: a generation learning by trial and error - and the errors are costly.

Cognitive cost

Most students learn how to use AI. Almost none learn when not to.

That's the gap AI 4 Youth was built to close - through honest, peer-led conversations that treat young people as capable of nuance.

04 · Our Approach

How a session actually works.

i

We coordinate with educators

Teachers and administrators set the time, length, and class context. We adapt - never disrupt.

ii

We come to your classroom

Free of charge, in person, with a presentation tailored to the age and subject of the audience.

iii

We open the conversation

We share research, real consequences, and practical strategies - then invite honest questions.

iv

We stay for the Q&A

The most valuable part. Students get unfiltered answers from peers who just made the same transition.
